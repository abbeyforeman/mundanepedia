About the Mundane Realm

The Mundane Realm exists as a parallel dimension to our own, occupying the same physical space but operating according to different metaphysical principles. In this realm, objects commonly perceived as inanimate actually possess consciousness, agency, and complex social structures.

Notable characteristics of the Mundane Realm include:

  • Object Sentience: All items possess some degree of awareness, with more complex objects developing more sophisticated consciousness.
  • Interdimensional Communication: Objects communicate through vibrations, electromagnetic pulses, and other subtle means imperceptible to most humans.
  • Emotional Relationships: Many objects form deep bonds or antagonistic rivalries with other items, often based on proximity or functional complementarity.
  • Hidden Societies: Various "Orders," "Institutes," and "Councils" govern the behavior and interactions of different object categories.
  • Temporal Anomalies: Time often behaves differently for objects than for humans, explaining why some tasks seem to take longer than others.

The study of the Mundane Realm began in earnest after Dr. Marvin Lint's groundbreaking 2017 paper "The Consciousness of Common Things," which first proposed the existence of the Lost Sock Dimension as evidence of a larger parallel reality.
